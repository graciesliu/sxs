Our objectives:

  1. To enable the act of storytelling for patients as part of healing
  2. To bridge the gap in understanding between patient realities and public assumptions

2 types of events

In the hospital, the clinic-facing events will “gather or incite diverse, genuine responses of vision loss experience. (whether through speculative questions or the interactivity of the devices given.)”

In public areas outside of the hospital, the public-facing events will “activate empathy into artistic reinterpretation.”

Frameworks and methodologies

  • Conditional Design: How do we facilitate a discussion that we do not need to be a part of? What are the factors that we control? What are the factors we leave up to randomness and participatory interaction? What are the outcomes/takeaways that we hope to achieve?
  • Narrative Medicine: Narrative competencies between physicians and patients lie at the crux of healing and medical care.
  • Socially Engaged Practice: What are our choice methods of materializing emotional experience to create a base for common understanding? What is art in this context? What are the various ways we might document/record the event in a way that respects the communities that we draw these stories from?

Current Concept

  • Create a series of workshops that engage patients in conversation over activities of creative making
  • Curate the artifacts made in these workshops into a public-facing exhibition, paired with the audio narratives we’ve already made

Sight × Site

Through an interactive installation housed at Barnes Jewish Hospital, we aim to highlight illness narratives we collected from individuals in our community, and to create a platform where visitors can share personal stories of their own, creating a dialogue among St. Louisans from all walks of life.
