Our Objectives

To enable the act of storytelling for patients as part of healing

To bridge the gap in understanding between patient realities and public assumptions

2 Types of Events

In the hospital, the clinic-facing events will gather or incite diverse, genuine responses of vision loss experience.

In public areas outside of the hospital, the public-facing events will activate empathy into artistic reinterpretation.

Frameworks & Methodologies

Conditional Design: How do we facilitate a discussion that we do not need to be a part of? What are the factors that we control? What are the factors we leave up to randomness and participatory interaction? What are the outcomes that we hope to achieve?

Narrative Medicine: Narrative competencies between physicians and patients lie at the crux of healing and medical care.

Socially Engaged Practice: What are our choice methods of materializing emotional experience to create a base for common understanding? What is art in this context? What are the various ways we might document/record the event in a way that respects the communities that we draw these stories from?

Current Concept

Create a series of workshops that engage patients in conversation over activities of creative making

Curate the artifacts made in these workshops into a public-facing exhibition, paired with the audio narratives we’ve already made

Iterative Making

We are continuing to learn as we go. Each sketch, prototype, event, and conversation with stakeholders helps us hone this project into better serving the community that we're engaging with.

About

We are a team of art, design, architecture, and engineering students in collaboration with WashU Medical School’s Barnes-Jewish Hospital Ophthalmology Clinic to develop and produce multi-sensory sited experiences that aim to engage people empathically with stories from the visually impaired community, exploring the intersection of health and design through narrative medicine.
