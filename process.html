Our Question of Investigation

WHAT IS THE POTENTIAL FUNCTION OF ART IN SPACES OF MEDICINE AND HEALING?

Patient interviews & Clinic Shadowing

Interviews with patients at the Barnes Jewish Hospital Ophthalmology Clinic have helped us recognize the assumptions from other that the visually impaired encounter in their daily lives and what they wished other people knew about them.

Shadowing at the clinic gave us a firsthand understanding of the doctor/patient relationship as well as the eye patients' navigation through the sphere of healthcare.

06.14.19 ERAF Diagram

Insights at Large

After processing our interviews by observing similiarities and differences throughout patient experiences, we've identified a few insights to investigate through the process of the project:

  • The fear of the unknown is amplified by the stakes of losing one’s vision.
  • From within, a sense of social isolation. For many, there may be no external signifier of their disability so people treat them based on their assumptions, these default explanations we have for the way people act.
  • Adaptation lies underneath the entire experience - physical, social, emotional
  • Loss: pain and frustration vs. Gain: perspective and gratitude
  • The visually impaired are just like the rest of us in our hopes and desires

Constraints & Considerations

HIPAA & patient anonymity VS raw/unfiltered content & emotional immediacy

  • What are our choice methods of materializing emotional experience to create a base for common understanding?
  • What is art in this context?
  • What are the various ways we might document/record the event?

Sensitivity towards our potential intrusiveness of the space & commodification of patient experiences

  • What makes for a comforting physical and conversation space?
  • What are the questions we should ask?
  • What are the visual and tactile cues that could be present?

Feedback & Critique

Through seeking out mentorship within the St. Louis community, we have been able improve our project through a professional lens. Our conversations with the designers, architects, and storytellers at PGAV Destination has encouraged us to consider the following:

  • What’s your competition? In a hospital, they are receptiveness and time
  • When do your participants engage with your experience? Visiting the clinic is already a whole experience, and depending on when they experience the installation, they will be in various modes of receptiveness and time-constrained
  • Experience is a choreography, there should be slow and fast parts, place where people dwell and places where people move through more quickly
  • Being placed in the clinic, how can the exchange of narrative in a public space like a hospital emotionally complement the healing of medical care?

Sight × Site

About

Sight by Site is an art and medicine collective based at Washington University in St. Louis. Our team includes physicians, medical students, artists, architects, engineers, and designers. In our work, we gather narratives of illness from people in our community and transform them into experiential installations, where visitors are invited to engage with these stories and share their own.
